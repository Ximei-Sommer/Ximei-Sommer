In summary, most of the work focus on the binary classification task about whether the post is with suicidal intent or not. Several studies are intended to classify examined posts into different categories related to suicide and mental health. There's one work focusing on predicting whether users who have mental health issues are going to develop suicidal thoughts.

Regarding the level of analysis, most of the studies focus on the post level.

In summary, the datasets developed for this task consist of posts collected from Reddit and/or Twitter. Most of them are designed for binary classification problems: determining whether a post expresses suicidal intent or whether the individual is at risk of attempting suicide.

The remaining three studies differ from the works above. One aims to classify examined posts into categories related to suicide; another focuses on predicting whether users with mental health issues are at risk of developing suicidal thoughts; and the last categorizes suicide risk into four classes while also identifying individuals who had not previously been recognized as at risk.

Regarding the level of analysis, half of the studies focus on the post level, while the others focus on the user level.
